Tracking your success across Google's local calling ecosystem involves using specific dashboards for organic profile interactions, AI-driven inquiries, and paid lead generation.1. Tracking Google Business Profile (GBP) InteractionsTo see how many people are finding and calling you organically, use the Performance dashboard in your Google Business Profile.How to access: Search for your business name on Google or Maps and select Performance from the merchant menu.Key Metrics: You can view a breakdown of interactions over the last six months, including:Calls: The number of times users tapped the "Call" button on your profile.Messages: If enabled, you can see the volume and your average response time, which Google tracks to measure business reliability.Direction & Website Clicks: Insights into how many users intended to visit your physical location or learn more online.Note: As of July 2024, Google phased out its native Call History/Chat feature, meaning you can no longer see a log of missed or answered calls directly within the GBP dashboard. You must now rely on your own phone records or a third-party call tracking tool to see specific caller IDs.2. Monitoring "Google Calling" (AI Agent) LeadsWhen Google’s AI (Duplex) calls your store to verify product availability or pricing for a customer, it acts as a "personal assistant" for the searcher.Identifying the Call: You will often hear an automated announcement stating that the call is from "Google" or a "Google Automated Assistant".Performance Impact: While there isn't a standalone "AI Call Dashboard" for merchants yet, these interactions directly influence your visibility. Responsive businesses that provide accurate pricing and stock info are more likely to be featured in AI-generated "quotes" sent to the customer via email or text.Missed Opportunities: Customers receive a "Businesses we couldn't reach" section in their summary. If your business appears here frequently, it can lead to a disadvantage against faster competitors.3. Tracking Paid Leads (Local Services Ads)If you use Local Services Ads (LSA), tracking is more robust because you pay per lead.The Lead Inbox: Access your LSA dashboard to see a complete list of every customer who called or messaged you.Call Recording & Logs: Google provides call recordings and duration logs for every LSA lead so you can review lead quality.Marking "Booked": To help Google understand your conversion rate, you should manually mark leads as "Booked" in the dashboard. This data helps Google optimize your ad placement to find more high-intent customers.Tracking Numbers: Google uses Google Forwarding Numbers on your ads.
