General Principles for First Impressions:
Conciseness: You have only a few seconds to capture attention (0:00-0:03). If users can't understand what you do quickly, they will leave (0:13-0:17).
Clarity: The main message should be immediately apparent. Avoid making the user "think" or guess what your product/service does (1:45-1:47).
Eliminate Noise: Remove anything that distracts from the core message or creates confusion (2:26-2:28).
Headline and Messaging:
Single, Clear Headline: The most important information should be above the fold and summarized in one clear headline, one byline, and one call to action (1:58-2:04).
Avoid Jargon: Use simple, accessible language. If your target audience uses specific jargon, test if it truly resonates with them (7:06-7:08, 10:09-10:18).
Problem & Solution: Clearly communicate the problem you solve and how you solve it, along with the user benefit (9:02-9:05).
Concise Phrasing: For animated or moving text, phrases should be short and easy to digest quickly (7:25-7:30).
Visual Design and Layout:
Hierarchy: Clearly establish a visual hierarchy so users know where to focus (1:28-1:30, 2:07-2:18). Avoid equally weighted columns if one message is more important.
Call to Actions (CTAs): Limit the number of CTAs to avoid the "paradox of choice," where too many options lead to no action (2:36-2:51).
Strategic Use of Space: Utilize empty space effectively to communicate more or excite the user, rather than leaving it blank (10:34-10:44, 10:55-10:57).
Legibility: Ensure text is easy to read. Avoid small, light grey text on dark backgrounds or busy backgrounds (8:42-8:48, 10:57-11:05).
Product Visualization: Show what the product looks like if it's a physical or tangible item (13:29-13:38). Clarify if it's a new product, an add-on, hardware, or software (13:42-13:52).
Animation and Motion:
Purposeful Animation: Animation should guide the user's eye to specific, important information or help illustrate complex concepts that are hard to convey with static elements (5:09-5:12, 5:36-5:47, 12:06-12:17).
Avoid Distraction: Motion should not distract from the main message (6:39-6:43, 6:55-7:00).
Simplicity in Animation: Keep animated illustrations simple and quickly explanatory (15:43-15:49).
Pacing: Avoid fast-moving animations that prevent users from processing information or understanding context if they join midway (16:02-16:13).
Meaningful Details: Ensure all details in animations contribute to understanding, rather than just being busy or confusing (16:42-16:58, 17:17-17:22).
Technical Considerations:
Page Speed: Optimize images and overall website weight for fast loading, especially on mobile devices. Google prioritizes faster sites in search rankings (19:34-20:02, 20:09-20:13, 20:23-20:28). A slow loading page means users will leave before seeing the content (20:31-20:37).
Pre-loaders: Ensure any pre-loaders are worth the wait; if the content isn't impactful, eliminate the pre-loader (10:44-10:52).

This video from Y Combinator's Design Review series features YC’s Aaron Epstein and Visiting Partner Raphael Schaad (founder of Cron). They analyze several user-submitted websites built with AI tools to identify common design pitfalls and best practices. The central theme is avoiding "vibe coding"—sites that look modern but feel generic, clunky, or dysfunctional due to overuse of AI-generated trends.
Common Pitfalls in AI-Designed Sites
Overuse of Trends: Ubiquitous use of purple gradients and similar accent colors makes sites look unoriginal (2:01).
Distracting Animations: Features like lines following the cursor or unnecessary fade-ins often distract from content rather than enhancing it (3:37, 13:35).
Complex Interactions: Scroll jacking (hijacking native browser scrolling) is frequently used, resulting in a clunky, slow user experience (13:41, 30:33).
Functional Bugs: AI-generated hover effects sometimes cause elements to disappear or behave unexpectedly, harming usability (5:53, 33:01).
Best Practices and Takeaways
Intentional Design: While AI tools make development fast, human judgment is essential to edit and refine the output (20:51).
Focus on Clarity: Prioritize clear messaging above the fold, ensuring visitors immediately understand the value proposition (12:13, 31:07).
Quality over Quantity: Focus on high-resolution assets and functional, tasteful hover effects (e.g., subtle pops) rather than complex animations that do not add value (4:49, 15:31).
Consider Mobile: Remember that hover effects do not translate to mobile devices, making them an unreliable way to display critical information (8:11).
